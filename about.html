I'm a product design engineer, which mostly means I love taking something from "what if" all the way to the "real deal" — talking to the people who'll use it, sketching and prototyping, sweating the CAD, working with vendors, and seeing it out the door. I like owning the whole messy arc, not just one slice of it.

My roots are in mechanical engineering (MIT — MEng in advanced manufacturing & design, and a BS in mechanical engineering with a concentration in product design), but what actually lights me up is the spot where engineering meets people — where a well-made thing quietly makes someone's day a little better.

I'm curious to a fault. I'll fall down a rabbit hole learning how espresso machines or record players or matcha bowls actually work, and then I'll want to make my own version. That shows up all over my life — a little home-cafe series I host, pottery I'm just getting into, cooking, whatever's next. However it looks on the surface, it's always the same thing underneath: figuring out how something works, then making the experience around it feel warm and effortless for the people it's for.

If any of that resonates, I'd love to hear from you.
